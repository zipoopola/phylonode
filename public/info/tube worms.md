This family of worms includes worms that live in deep sea vents and uses symbiotic bacteria for nutrition, and bone worms, which also gain nutrition through the breakdown of collagen in bones of animals via symbiotic bacteria. These are different types of bacteria, and it is believed the ancestral condition for the group was the use of chemosynthetic bacteria.

As larvae, these worms have mouths and a simple gut, but as adults, they rely entirely on their respective bacterium. Vent worms capture H2S from the vents with their large fan-like gills, and bone worms have root-like structures.

Interestingly, a member of the closest relatives of these two groups, the feather-duster worms, that lives near deep-sea methane seeps, and gets much of its nutrition from methane-digesting symbiotic bacteria. This demonstrates this family has a wider relationship with deep-sea bacteria.
