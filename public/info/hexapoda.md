The fossil record has a large Hexapod Gap between 470 MYA and 410 MYA, which unfortunately is the time in which hexapods moved onto land. A 2023 paper discusses a new potential oldest hexapod member from Castle Bank, Wales from 462 MYA, with the next oldest being a primitive springtail from the Devonian, 410 MYA. This is a marine organism, helping to bridge that gap.

These primitive hexapods had fully musculated antennae, like other crustaceans, whereas true insects only have muscular antenna bases. They also had no waxy outer layer, meaning they were reliant on water.
